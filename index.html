Me.

    Ian Deans is a software developer in the San Francisco Bay area. He has been programming casually from young age and has been building web applications in a professional environment for the last three years. In those years Ian has built and contributed to several open source software projects, working alone and in pair with other developers.

    Ian's exposure to computers at an early age was due to his father being a network technician and self-taught programmer in the 1980's. He helped his father put together home networks with proxy severs in the 90's and build an early version of the website for the consulting company his father worked for. Throughout junior high and high school, Ian dabbled in modding games, building MUDs, and constructing simple websites with HTML.

    Before stepping into the professional software world, Ian decided to turn away from computer programming and pursued a career in sound engineering in the Los Angeles area. Do to lack of financial support, he eventually moved to the San Francisco Bay area to enroll in school and pursue the career he was meant for. Again, financial struggles slowed Ian's progress; but with the emergence of Udacity and then the forming of Learner's Guild mixed with his self-motivation and dedication, he acquired the skills to match his talents and make him a powerful developer.

My Education.

    My educational background is pretty untraditional, but through a mixture of self-teaching with tutorials and books, online courses, Udacity Nanodegrees, and an apprenticeship at Learner's Guild; not to mention the various experiences from the different jobs I've held throughout supporting my education; I have gained a plethora of skills that allow me to excel, not only in my own projects, but in team environments as well.

Learner's Guild

    Learner's Guild is a 40 week apprenticeship program in Oakland, CA. It is a place where people with some experience in programming can take on web development as a new skill, or refine existing ones. Focused on building full-stack applications in Node.js in a professional environment. Learner's Guild uses weekly development cycles paired with code shipping goals and deadlines to drive apprentices to learn through experience. There are on-site and remote professionals that lead a couple of open source projects as well as offer assistance and workshops on various technologies and practices. Here apprentices learn not only to build and ship quality code while in team, but also how to assertively give and take feedback as a way to improve team relations, efficiency, and productivity.

  • Built dozens of small to mid-scale applications
  • Contributed to open source projects Trossello and STEP
  • Was lead coach to multiple teams of apprentices
  • Designed, developed, and deployed apps to live production
